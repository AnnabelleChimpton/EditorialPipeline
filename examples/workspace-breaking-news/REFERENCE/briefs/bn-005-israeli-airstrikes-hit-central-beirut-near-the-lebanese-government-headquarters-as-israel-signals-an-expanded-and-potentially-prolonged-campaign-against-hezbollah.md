## Event
Israeli airstrikes hit central Beirut near the Lebanese government headquarters as Israel signals an expanded and potentially prolonged campaign against Hezbollah

## Recommended format
- analysis brief

## Recommended platform
- newsletter / long-form

## Why this format
- The dossier is not just a sequence of strikes; it shows a meaningful shift in targeting, political signaling, and humanitarian pressure. An analysis brief is the better fit because the core value is explaining what changes when Israeli strikes move from Hezbollah-linked southern suburbs into central Beirut near Lebanese state institutions.

## Angle / thesis
- The key development is that Israel’s campaign in Lebanon now appears to be broadening from pressure on Hezbollah strongholds to a more politically consequential strategy that places central Beirut, displacement hubs, and Lebanese state authority under direct strain.

## Key facts to include
- Reuters reported that on 2026-03-12 an Israeli strike hit Beirut’s Bachoura neighbourhood around 1 km from Lebanon’s Grand Serail government headquarters, followed by another strike in nearby Zuqaq al-Blat after warnings to residents.
- Reuters and ABC News both cited Israeli officials signaling a longer campaign, with Lieutenant General Eyal Zamir saying, “This operation will not be short,” and Israel Defense Minister Israel Katz saying the military was preparing to expand activity in Lebanon.
- UN News reported on 2026-03-12 that 815,000 people had been uprooted in Lebanon since the current escalation began, with around 120,000 staying in collective shelters, many in public schools.
- Reuters quoted UN Resident and Humanitarian Coordinator in Lebanon Imran Riza saying, “The cost on civilians is too high,” while UN News quoted him calling the crisis a “full-blown humanitarian catastrophe.”
- Reuters reported that the central Beirut strikes followed Hezbollah’s largest rocket-and-drone barrage of the current conflict, which Israel said occurred alongside Iranian ballistic missile fire.

## What to exclude
- Do not turn the piece into a full chronology of every exchange since 2026-03-02; use only the minimum timeline needed to support the thesis.
- Do not present Israeli claims about Hezbollah targets or battlefield success as independently verified fact.
- Do not over-focus on precise casualty totals where source counts differ and remain in motion.
- Do not drift into broad history of Israel-Hezbollah relations beyond the context needed to explain why central Beirut is a significant escalation.

## Sensitivity flags
- Central Beirut includes civilian, governmental, and displacement-linked spaces; avoid language that normalizes strikes in urban civilian areas.
- Casualty and displacement figures are still moving; attribute every number to Reuters, UN News, the Lebanese health ministry, or other named sources.
- The reported coordination between Hezbollah and Iran should remain source-attributed, not stated as settled operational fact.
- Avoid graphic descriptions of people sleeping in tents or at strike sites; the humanitarian point is scale and vulnerability, not vivid detail.
- Be careful not to collapse Lebanese state institutions, Hezbollah infrastructure, and civilian sheltering into one undifferentiated target environment.

## Confidence qualifiers needed
- Use “according to Reuters” for the exact location and timing of the Bachoura and Zuqaq al-Blat strikes.
- Use “Israel said” or “according to Israeli officials, as cited by Reuters/ABC” for claims about Hezbollah facilities, simultaneous Iranian fire, and the operational purpose of strikes.
- Use “according to Lebanon’s health ministry, as cited by Reuters/ABC” for casualty figures from Beirut strikes and national death tolls.
- Use “UN News reported” for the 815,000 displacement figure and shelter numbers.
- Note that the strongest sources are same-day and relatively fresh, but casualty/displacement figures may still move quickly before publication.
