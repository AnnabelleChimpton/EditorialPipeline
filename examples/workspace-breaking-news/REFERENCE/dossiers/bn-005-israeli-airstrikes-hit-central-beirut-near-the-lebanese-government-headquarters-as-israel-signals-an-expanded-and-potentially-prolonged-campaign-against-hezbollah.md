## Event
Israeli airstrikes hit central Beirut near the Lebanese government headquarters as Israel signals an expanded and potentially prolonged campaign against Hezbollah

## Confidence
- confirmed

## Urgency
- critical

## Timeline of developments
- 2026-03-02 — Hezbollah opened fire on Israel, saying it was acting in response to the killing of Iran’s supreme leader, according to Reuters’ 2026-03-12 reconstruction of the current Lebanon front. Israel then launched a widened offensive against Hezbollah in Lebanon. (Reuters, 2026-03-12)
- 2026-03-10 — Reuters reported, citing UN agencies, that the humanitarian crisis in Lebanon had deepened, with more than 667,000 people displaced and dozens of children killed as Israeli strikes and evacuation orders intensified. (Reuters, 2026-03-10)
- 2026-03-12 15:30 GMT — Reuters reported that an Israeli airstrike hit a building in Beirut’s Bachoura neighbourhood around 1 km from Lebanon’s Grand Serail government headquarters. A second strike then hit nearby Zuqaq al-Blat after an Israeli warning to residents. (Reuters, 2026-03-12)
- 2026-03-12 — Reuters said a pre-dawn strike in another area of Beirut killed 12 people, according to Lebanon’s health ministry, after hitting a seafront sidewalk where displaced families had been sleeping in tents. (Reuters, 2026-03-12)
- 2026-03-12 — Israel Defense Minister Israel Katz said the military had orders to expand the campaign in Lebanon, while Israeli military chief Lieutenant General Eyal Zamir said: “This operation will not be short. We will bring additional troops and capabilities to the north.” (Reuters and ABC News, 2026-03-12)
- 2026-03-12 — UN News quoted UN Resident and Humanitarian Coordinator in Lebanon Imran Riza describing the crisis as a “full-blown humanitarian catastrophe,” saying 815,000 people had been uprooted since the current escalation began. (UN News, 2026-03-12)
- 2026-03-12 — Reuters quoted Riza saying “The cost on civilians is too high” and warning that displacement would continue if the fighting widened further. (Reuters, 2026-03-12)

## Background & context
- This escalation sits inside the wider regional war involving Israel, Iran, Hezbollah, and U.S. forces, but the immediate trigger for the current Lebanon front was Hezbollah’s 2026-03-02 rocket fire into Israel, followed by a sharply intensified Israeli campaign.
- The current phase differs from earlier Lebanon fighting because strikes are no longer concentrated mainly on southern Lebanon and Beirut’s southern suburbs. The move into Bachoura and Zuqaq al-Blat brings central Beirut, near state institutions and diplomatic areas, more directly into the target set.
- The displacement context matters. Many Lebanese families had only recently returned after earlier 2024 displacement, according to UN News, and have now been forced to flee again under broader evacuation orders.
- The dossier also needs to be read against the widening regional war: Reuters and other reporting tie Hezbollah’s biggest salvo to coordinated pressure alongside Iranian strikes, though those specific battlefield coordination claims remain attributed, not independently verified here.

## Key actors & positions
- Israel Defense Minister Israel Katz: Reuters quoted Katz saying, “We promised quiet and security to the communities of the north, and that is exactly what we will deliver.” ABC News separately quoted him saying he and the prime minister instructed the Israel Defense Forces to prepare to expand operations in Lebanon.
- Lieutenant General Eyal Zamir, Israeli military chief: Reuters and ABC reported Zamir saying the Lebanon operation “will not be short” and that additional troops and capabilities would be moved north.
- Israeli military: According to Reuters, the military said it warned residents before the central Beirut strikes and said Hezbollah had launched its prior attack simultaneously with Iran. ABC quoted the IDF as saying it was striking Hezbollah infrastructure in Beirut.
- Hezbollah: Reuters reported Hezbollah launched its largest rocket and drone barrage of the current conflict on the previous day. The group has continued daily fire into Israel since 2026-03-02, according to Reuters’ account.
- Imran Riza, UN Resident and Humanitarian Coordinator in Lebanon: Reuters and UN News quoted Riza warning that the civilian burden is extreme. He told Reuters, “The cost on civilians is too high,” and told UN News the crisis had become a “full-blown humanitarian catastrophe.”
- Lebanese authorities: Reuters cited Lebanon’s health ministry on reported casualty totals and on deaths from the pre-dawn Beirut strike. Lebanese officials also provided the displacement and evacuation-impact figures carried by multiple outlets.

## Humanitarian / civilian dimension
- Reuters reported on 2026-03-12 that Lebanese authorities said 687 people had been killed in Israeli attacks, including 98 children, 62 women, and 18 medics. Reuters noted the toll did not otherwise distinguish between civilians and combatants.
- UN News reported on 2026-03-12 that 815,000 people had been uprooted in Lebanon since the current escalation began on 2026-03-02. It said around 120,000 displaced people were staying in collective shelters, most of them in public schools.
- Reuters reported that more than 800,000 people had fled their homes under Israeli strikes and evacuation orders. That figure aligns broadly with the UN’s same-day picture, though the exact total varies slightly by source and methodology.
- Reuters reported that a pre-dawn strike in Beirut killed 12 people on a seafront sidewalk where displaced families had been sleeping in tents. ABC separately cited Lebanon’s Ministry of Public Health as saying at least eight people died in strikes on Beirut’s Corniche area.
- The humanitarian significance of the central Beirut strikes is not only casualties. They also indicate that areas sheltering displaced civilians and districts close to core state institutions are now within the active strike pattern.

## International response
- Reuters reported that Imran Riza, speaking as the UN’s senior humanitarian official in Lebanon, called on UN member states to press belligerents to respect international humanitarian law.
- UN News amplified the humanitarian warning on 2026-03-12, framing the Lebanon emergency as part of a broader regional crisis that is now overwhelming shelter capacity, education access, and civilian coping mechanisms.
- No major new ceasefire or Security Council action specific to the 2026-03-12 central Beirut strikes was confirmed in the sources reviewed for this dossier.
- The most visible international response in the current sourcing is therefore humanitarian and legal rather than diplomatic breakthrough: warnings about displacement scale, civilian harm, and compliance with the laws of war.

## Analysis: what this means
- The central development is that Israel appears to be normalizing strikes beyond Hezbollah’s traditional strongholds in Beirut’s southern suburbs and into central districts near major Lebanese state institutions. That expands the political and symbolic scope of the campaign, not just its geography.
- Public comments from Katz and Zamir matter because they frame the Beirut strikes as part of a longer campaign rather than a one-off response. When paired with wider evacuation orders, they suggest Israel is preparing for sustained pressure on Hezbollah across more of Lebanon.
- The humanitarian data gives this escalation additional weight. A front that already displaced roughly 800,000 people is now intersecting more directly with central Beirut, where state, diplomatic, and displacement dynamics overlap.
- The practical implication is that any next round of Hezbollah fire could trigger an even broader Israeli target set, while the Lebanese state faces increasing pressure as areas closer to government authority and international presence come under attack.

## What to watch
- Whether additional strikes hit central Beirut districts beyond Bachoura and Zuqaq al-Blat, especially near government buildings, embassies, or UN facilities.
- Whether Hezbollah launches another mass rocket-and-drone salvo, and whether Israel responds with deeper or broader strikes inside Beirut.
- Updated casualty and displacement figures from the Lebanese health ministry, UN agencies, and humanitarian responders, especially for central Beirut.
- Any shift from humanitarian warnings to formal diplomatic action at the UN Security Council or by key mediators.
- Whether Israeli evacuation orders expand further north or are paired with additional troop movements suggesting a ground escalation.
