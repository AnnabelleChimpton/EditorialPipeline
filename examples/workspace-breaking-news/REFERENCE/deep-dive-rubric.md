# DEEP-DIVE SITUATION DOSSIER RUBRIC

This rubric is for the deep-dive pass — converting a triaged event card into a full situation dossier with context, analysis, and forward-looking assessment.

## Goal
Produce a full dossier using `topic-dossier-template.md` that is meaningfully richer than the event card: more sources, historical context, actor analysis, humanitarian dimension, and analytical assessment.

## Minimum acceptance bar
A situation dossier is acceptable only if it includes:
- 5-8 sources total, at least 2 from wire services or official statements
- All template sections filled (no blank sections)
- Chronological timeline with timestamps on every entry
- At least 1 direct quote from an official statement or named official
- Key actors section with named individuals, official titles, and attributed positions
- Humanitarian dimension addressed (even if "no civilian impact data available at this time")
- Analysis section grounded in facts from the timeline, not speculation
- "What to watch" section with specific, testable forward-looking indicators

## What "goes beyond" the event card means
- New sources not already cited in the event card
- Historical context: what prior events, agreements, or patterns inform this development
- Actor analysis: motivations, constraints, stated positions — attributed, not assumed
- Multiple perspectives: what different parties say happened, noted as their accounts
- Specific data points: casualty figures, displacement numbers, damage assessments — sourced to agencies
- At least one detail or angle the event card didn't cover

## Research targets (2-3 web searches)
Prioritize:
- Official statements (government, military, UN, ICRC) not found in the event card
- Wire service updates published after the event card's sources
- UN agency situation reports (OCHA, UNRWA, WHO) for humanitarian data
- Background context: prior agreements, relevant UN resolutions, precedent operations
- Eyewitness accounts from named journalists on the ground

## Anti-slop rules
Do not pass a dossier as acceptable if it:
- Rehashes the event card with more words but no new substance
- Fills the analysis section with "the situation is complex" or similar vacuities
- Presents speculation as analysis (analysis must be grounded in cited facts)
- Uses "both sides" without naming the specific actors and their specific positions
- Provides false balance — if the evidence clearly points one direction, don't manufacture a counterpoint
- Has a timeline that isn't actually chronological or lacks timestamps
- Pads the source list with near-duplicate articles from the same outlet
- Uses emotional language in place of factual reporting ("heartbreaking," "horrifying," "shocking")
