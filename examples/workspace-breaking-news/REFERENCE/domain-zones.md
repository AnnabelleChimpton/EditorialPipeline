# Domain Zones — Middle East Conflict Monitoring

Thematic zones for conflict coverage. The sourcing agent reads this file and uses it to ensure diverse coverage across the full domain.

---

## 1. Military operations & security
Active combat, airstrikes, ground operations, weapons use, military deployments, ceasefire violations, escalation/de-escalation patterns. Includes both state militaries and non-state armed groups.

**Source priority:** Official military statements, verified open-source intelligence, wire service reporting from the ground, defense ministry releases.

## 2. Diplomatic negotiations & international response
Peace talks, ceasefire proposals, UN resolutions, bilateral meetings, mediator statements, international coalition positions. Includes both formal negotiations and back-channel diplomacy.

**Source priority:** Official statements from foreign ministries, UN press releases, joint communiqués, named diplomatic sources.

## 3. Humanitarian situation & civilian impact
Civilian casualties, displacement, infrastructure damage, aid access, medical capacity, food/water security, child welfare, protected sites (hospitals, schools, refugee camps). UNRWA, ICRC, MSF, WHO reporting.

**Source priority:** UN agency reports, ICRC/Red Crescent updates, MSF field reports, WHO health cluster bulletins, named aid workers on the ground.

## 4. Political dynamics (domestic actors)
Internal political maneuvering by all parties — government coalitions, opposition movements, factional splits, public opinion shifts, protests, elections, leadership changes.

**Source priority:** Local reporting in original language (translated), official party/faction statements, polling data, named political analysts.

## 5. International law & accountability
ICC proceedings, ICJ rulings, war crimes allegations and investigations, arms transfer legality, occupation law, protected status violations, humanitarian law compliance.

**Source priority:** Court filings and rulings, UN Commission of Inquiry reports, human rights organization investigations (HRW, Amnesty, B'Tselem), legal scholars.

## 6. Media & information environment
Journalist safety, press access restrictions, social media dynamics, disinformation campaigns, information blackouts, narrative warfare, platform content moderation decisions.

**Source priority:** Committee to Protect Journalists, Reporters Without Borders, platform transparency reports, journalist first-person accounts.

## 7. Economic impact & sanctions
Sanctions regimes, economic blockades, reconstruction costs, energy markets, trade disruptions, aid funding decisions, financial system access.

**Source priority:** Treasury/sanctions authority announcements, World Bank/IMF assessments, trade data, energy market reporting.

## 8. Historical context & precedent
Prior conflicts, agreements, and resolutions that inform the current situation. Oslo Accords, Camp David, prior Gaza operations, UN partition plan, settlement history, refugee right of return.

**Source priority:** Academic sources, official treaty texts, UN archives, established historical accounts. Use sparingly — context enriches coverage but should not displace current reporting.
