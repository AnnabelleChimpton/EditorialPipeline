## What happened
- Reuters reported on 2026-03-12 that Israeli airstrikes hit two buildings in central Beirut near Lebanon's Grand Serail government headquarters, including one strike in Bachoura at about 1530 GMT and another in nearby Zuqaq al-Blat after evacuation warnings.
- Reuters, ABC News, and The Guardian each reported on 2026-03-12 that the strikes came as Israel expanded evacuation orders in southern Lebanon and signaled that its campaign against Hezbollah would widen and not be short.
- According to Reuters, the strikes followed Hezbollah's largest rocket and drone barrage of the current Lebanon front, which Israel said had been carried out alongside Iranian ballistic missile fire.
- The strike pattern marks a further move from attacks concentrated on Beirut's southern suburbs toward central districts close to state institutions and areas sheltering displaced civilians.

## Key facts
- Reuters said one strike hit Bachoura around 1530 GMT about 1 km from the Lebanese government's Grand Serail headquarters.
- Reuters and The Guardian reported that Israel issued warnings before striking buildings in central Beirut neighborhoods.
- ABC News reported that Israel Defense Minister Israel Katz said the military was preparing to expand activity in Lebanon, while Eyal Zamir said, "This operation will not be short."
- Reuters and ABC both reported that new Israeli evacuation orders expanded the area in southern Lebanon from which residents were told to leave.

## Source corroboration
- Reuters provides the strongest on-the-ground reporting on the central Beirut strikes and timing.
- The Guardian independently corroborates the central Beirut strikes, the evacuation warnings, and the link to a broader Israeli escalation against Hezbollah.
- ABC News separately corroborates Israel's stated intention to expand operations in Lebanon and cites senior Israeli officials by name.
- The core facts are consistent across these outlets, though some casualty and targeting details vary by source and remain source-attributed.

## Conflicting claims
- Israel says it was targeting Hezbollah facilities and infrastructure, according to Reuters, The Guardian, and ABC's reporting of Israeli military statements.
- Civilian-impact details differ across reports. Reuters cites Lebanon's health ministry saying a pre-dawn Beirut strike killed 12 people, while ABC cites at least eight deaths in strikes on Beirut's Corniche area.
- Hezbollah and Iran-linked accounts, as reflected through secondary reporting, frame the broader exchange as coordinated resistance activity; this card does not independently verify those battlefield claims.

## Context needed
- Israel's Lebanon campaign intensified after Hezbollah opened fire on Israel on 2026-03-02, saying it was acting in response to the killing of Iran's supreme leader.
- Before this phase, much of the Israeli bombardment had focused on southern Lebanon, eastern Lebanon, and Hezbollah-linked southern suburbs of Beirut rather than central districts near key government sites.
- The expansion of evacuation orders and public statements from Israeli leaders suggest the Lebanon front is becoming a larger and potentially longer-running theater within the wider regional war involving Israel, Iran, and allied armed groups.
- Any deep-dive should track whether the center of Beirut is becoming a regular target set and how that changes civilian displacement and Lebanese state calculations.
