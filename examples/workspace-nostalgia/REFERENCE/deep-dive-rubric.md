# DEEP-DIVE DOSSIER RUBRIC

This rubric is for the deep-dive pass — the second research layer that converts a "strong" first-pass card into an article-ready dossier.

## Goal
Produce a full dossier using `topic-dossier-template.md` that is meaningfully richer than the first-pass card: more sources, more texture, more specificity, and concrete post angles.

## Minimum acceptance bar
A deep-dive dossier is acceptable only if it includes:
- 6-10 sources total, at least 2 contemporaneous or primary
- All template sections filled (no blank sections)
- At least 2 strong pull quotes or memorable phrasing examples
- At least 1 named person, brand, or platform with specific detail beyond what the first-pass card covered
- Drama / humor / absurdity section has concrete incidents, not just vibes
- At least 2 distinct future post angles with enough detail to start drafting from
- Material that goes beyond the first-pass card — new facts, new sources, new angles

## What "goes beyond" means
- New sources not already cited in the first-pass card
- Specific incidents, dates, names, or numbers the card didn't have
- Forum posts, blog entries, contemporary news coverage, or archived material
- Pull quotes from people who were there, not retrospective summaries
- At least one detail weird or specific enough to surprise a reader who lived through the era

## Research targets (3-5 web searches per topic)
Prioritize:
- Contemporaneous news coverage, blog posts, or forum threads
- Specific people or incidents mentioned in the card's Upgrade path or Open question sections
- Archived material (Wayback Machine references, old blog posts, forum screenshots)
- Absurd, granular details that make the topic feel alive rather than summarized
- Pull-quotable material from participants, critics, or contemporary observers
- **Source texture**: Actively hunt for community-level artifacts: forum posts, user-created guides, archived comment threads, farewell posts, complaint pages, official platform documentation, personal blog posts. These give the draft first-person texture that press coverage cannot. A dossier built only on mainstream press (NYT, LAT, Wired) will produce secondhand-feeling writing.

## Anti-slop rules
Do not pass a dossier as acceptable if it:
- Rehashes the first-pass card with more words but no new substance
- Fills sections with generic era summary or "this was iconic" padding
- Uses retrospective listicle material as primary sourcing
- Has pull quotes that are actually paraphrases or AI-generated
- Lists post angles that are just restatements of the topic name
- Pads the source list with near-duplicate articles from the same outlet
