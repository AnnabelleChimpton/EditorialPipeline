## Topic
MySpace profile culture and the tyranny of the Top 8

## Verdict
- strong

## Fast summary
- MySpace turned teen social life into interface furniture: customizable profiles made identity feel theatrical, and the Top 8 made friendship ranking brutally visible. The topic has strong post potential because the product design itself manufactured status anxiety, petty warfare, and extremely public social math.

## Facts
- MySpace launched in 2003 and quickly became one of the defining social-network platforms of the mid-2000s.
- Profiles were heavily customizable with HTML/CSS, glitter graphics, autoplay music, and embedded media, which made the page itself part scrapbook, part nightclub flyer, part personal brand exercise.
- The Top Friends module displayed a ranked set of friends by default; “Top 8” became the cultural shorthand even when users could later expand the number.
- News Corp. bought MySpace’s parent company Intermix in 2005 for $580 million, a useful marker for how fast the site had become commercially and culturally central.
- In 2006, MySpace was reported as overtaking Google as the most visited website in the U.S. by Hitwise, which helps explain why its social rituals became so widely legible.
- danah boyd’s youth-social-network research treated public friend displays as a meaningful part of online identity performance, not just a cute feature.

## Mini timeline
- 2003: MySpace launches.
- 2005: News Corp. acquires Intermix/MySpace for $580 million.
- 2005-2006: Profile customization and Top 8 culture become mainstream teen internet behavior.
- 2006: Hitwise reports MySpace as the most visited U.S. website, cementing peak cultural saturation.
- 2007: Scholarly analysis starts clearly framing friend-ranking and profile display as social architecture, not neutral decoration.

## Cultural / platform angle
- The genius-horror of MySpace was that it collapsed friendship, aesthetics, and status into one public page. Top 8 was not just a feature; it was an algorithm-free ranking ritual that trained users to perform affiliation in public and interpret UI decisions as emotional truth.

## Drama / humor / absurdity angle
- Top 8 took normal adolescent politics and gave it a dashboard. You were not merely annoyed with your friend; you could demote her to slot #7 like a tiny velvet-rope dictator and let the whole school notice.
