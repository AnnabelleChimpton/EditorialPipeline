# FIRST-PASS DOSSIER RUBRIC

This rubric is for first-pass research packets, not final articles.

## Goal
Produce a compact dossier that is good enough to preserve, rank, and revisit later without pretending it is already publication-ready.

## Minimum acceptance bar
A first-pass dossier is acceptable only if it includes:
- 3-5 solid sources total
- at least 1 contemporaneous, primary, or primary-adjacent source when realistically available
- 5+ concrete facts
- a clear cultural / platform / [YOUR DOMAIN] angle
- at least 1 tension point: humor, absurdity, conflict, controversy, behavioral weirdness, or [YOUR DOMAIN]-specific tension
- explicit open questions or verification gaps
- a final verdict: `strong`, `maybe`, or `weak`

## Verdict meanings
### Strong
Use when:
- sources are sturdy enough for later expansion
- the topic has a distinct hook
- there is obvious post potential beyond surface-level coverage
- the dossier already contains enough texture to suggest a real argument or narrative

### Maybe
Use when:
- the topic is promising but the angle is still fuzzy
- sources exist but are thinner, repetitive, or too retrospective
- the material is interesting but not yet sharp enough to justify deepening

### Weak
Use when:
- sourcing is too thin or too flimsy
- the topic is mostly generic [YOUR DOMAIN] coverage with no strong tension
- the available material does not support a compelling future post

## Upgrade rules
A `maybe` topic can become `strong` if a later pass adds one or more of:
- a better primary/contemporaneous source
- a sharper narrative frame
- stronger drama, platform logic, or social meaning
- better factual specificity

## Anti-slop rules

### Universal (apply to all domains)
Do not pass a dossier as acceptable if it is mostly:
- generic [YOUR DOMAIN] summary
- listicle fluff
- repeated facts from one source rewritten five ways
- rumor without receipts
- vague superlatives with no explanation of why something mattered
